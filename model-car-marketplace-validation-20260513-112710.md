# Validation Playbook: Model Car Marketplace

Generated on 2026-05-13
Goal: Validate whether frequent model car sellers will move real inventory from Facebook groups into a dedicated marketplace.

## Validation Target

Talk to 10 frequent model car sellers, proxy buyers, or bulk traders.

The goal is not to ask whether they "like the idea." The goal is to find out whether they will take a real action:

- list 5 real items
- share marketplace links back into Facebook groups
- use transaction history as trust proof
- move part of the transaction away from Facebook

## Who To Contact

Prioritize sellers who match at least 2 of these:

- Posts model car listings weekly or more often
- Has sold multiple cars in the same Facebook group
- Sells higher-value or limited models
- Acts as a proxy buyer, reseller, or bulk trader
- Has buyers asking for proof, reputation, or past transaction records
- Already keeps inventory photos or price lists somewhere

Avoid starting with casual collectors who only buy once in a while. They are not the supply-side wedge.

## Outreach Message

Use this as the first private message:

```text
Hi, 我正在驗證一個模型車專用的買賣平台，想先找常交易的賣家聊 10 分鐘。

我看到現在很多交易都在 FB 社團完成，但資訊、狀態和賣家交易紀錄比較分散。

我不是要推銷東西，想問你 4 個問題：
1. 你現在賣模型車最麻煩的是哪一步？
2. 買家會不會要求看你的交易紀錄或信用證明？
3. 如果有一個模型車專用頁面，可以放庫存和成交紀錄，你會不會願意先放 5 台真實商品？
4. 如果這些商品連結可以貼回 FB 社團，你會不會用？

可以跟你約 10 分鐘嗎？
```

## Short Version For Warm Contacts

```text
我在驗證一個模型車 marketplace，想取代 FB 社團裡混亂的交易流程。

你是常交易的人，我想問你 10 分鐘：如果有賣家頁、商品刊登、成交紀錄，你會不會真的放 5 台車上去賣？
```

## Interview Script

Ask these in order. Do not explain the product too much before hearing their current workflow.

### 1. Current Workflow

How do you currently sell or exchange model cars?

Follow-ups:

- Which Facebook groups or channels do you use?
- How often do you post listings?
- How many items did you sell or trade in the last 30 days?
- What happens after someone comments or messages you?

### 2. Trust

How do buyers decide whether they can trust you?

Follow-ups:

- Do buyers ask for transaction records?
- Do they check your profile or past posts?
- Have you ever lost a deal because the buyer did not trust the transaction?
- What proof do you currently show when a buyer hesitates?

### 3. Listing Pain

What is annoying about listing in Facebook groups?

Follow-ups:

- Are posts hard to search later?
- Is item status hard to manage?
- Do buyers keep asking about sold or reserved items?
- Do you maintain inventory somewhere else already?

### 4. Marketplace Commitment

If a model-car-specific marketplace existed, would you list 5 real items this week?

Push for a concrete answer:

- Which 5 items?
- Would you upload real photos?
- Would you set real prices?
- Would you share those links back into Facebook groups?
- What would stop you?

### 5. Replacement Threshold

What would need to be true before you stop using Facebook groups as the main transaction place?

Follow-ups:

- More buyers?
- Easier listing?
- Better search?
- Seller transaction history?
- Lower scam risk?
- Better status management?

Score each seller:

- 3 = They will list 5 real items this week and share links back to Facebook.
- 2 = They are interested but need one missing feature or clearer buyer demand.
- 1 = They like the idea but will not take action.
- 0 = They do not have this problem.

## Decision Rule

Proceed with full marketplace MVP only if:

- At least 5 of 10 sellers score 3.
- At least 7 of 10 sellers report real trust or listing pain.
- At least 5 sellers agree to share marketplace links back into Facebook groups.
- At least 3 sellers can name specific items they would list immediately.

If results are weaker:

- If sellers want a profile but not marketplace browsing: build seller pages first.
- If sellers mainly want trust proof: build transaction history pages first.
- If buyers are the only ones with pain: do not build yet; find a stronger supply-side wedge.

## Red Flags

Treat these as weak demand:

- "Sounds interesting."
- "I might use it if there are many buyers."
- "Let me know when it is ready."
- "I do not want to upload listings twice."
- "Facebook is messy, but good enough."

Treat these as strong demand:

- "I can send you 5 items now."
- "I already keep a spreadsheet or album of inventory."
- "Buyers often ask if I have previous transactions."
- "I hate answering whether something is sold."
- "If this saves me from repeated questions, I will use it."
